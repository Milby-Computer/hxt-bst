<p></p>
<p>aeMorphGradientRamp changes the ramp of a fillgradient of an object. It allows you to use easing effects for the transition.</p>
<p></p>
<p>Example:</p>
<p></p>
<p>aeMorphGradientRamp the long ID of grc 1,tGradient1,tGradient2,3000,"overshoot"</p>
<p></p>
<p>Parameters:</p>
<p></p>
<p>   long ID of the graphic that changes the gradient</p>
<p>   startGradient</p>
<p>   endGradient</p>
<p>   duration in millisecs</p>
<p>   easing method (in, out, inOut, overshoot, bounce or empty)</p>
<p></p>
<p>See stack ae6demo to see it in action.</p>
<p></p>
<p>Attention! </p>
<p></p>
<p>Currently the gradientLists must hold an equal number of entries, otherwise the morphing of the graphics will break. If there is enough interested in this feature, I will try to explore means to change that behaviour.</p>
